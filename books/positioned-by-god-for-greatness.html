Introduction

There comes a moment in life when you begin to ask deeper questions. Why am I here? Why is my journey like this? Why do some things take longer while others seem to happen quickly?

Many people live without clear answers. They struggle through seasons of confusion, disappointment, and waiting. At times, it may feel as though life is moving without direction.

Yet there is a truth that changes everything. Your life is not random. You are not here by accident. You have been positioned by God for a purpose, and that purpose includes greatness.

Greatness is not only about success as the world defines it. It is about becoming who God created you to be. It is about living a life that reflects His power, His favor, and His intention.

This book was written to help you understand that your current situation does not define your future. What you are going through now is not the end of your story. It may be part of a process that is preparing you for something greater.

The Bible shows us again and again that before God brings people into visible greatness, He first positions them. Joseph was positioned through trials before he stood in the palace. David was positioned in obscurity before he sat on the throne. Esther was positioned in a foreign land before she became a voice of deliverance.

In the same way, your life is being arranged by God. Every experience, every delay, every challenge can become part of His plan. Nothing is wasted when it is placed in His hands.

As you read through these pages, you will discover simple truths that can guide your heart and strengthen your faith. You will see how God works behind the scenes. You will learn how to trust His timing. You will understand how to remain steady even when things seem uncertain.

This book will remind you that God is still at work in your life. It will help you see that what looks like a setback may be a setup for something greater. So take this journey with an open heart. Allow the word of God to speak to you. Let faith rise again within you. You are not forgotten. You are not left behind. You are positioned by God for greatness.

Chapter 1

Positioned To Advance The Vision

The story of my service and faith is closely woven into the early history and continuing global expansion of Mountain of Fire and Miracles Ministries (MFM). From humble beginnings of serving in the ministry's information unit during its formative years to becoming a respected deliverance minister and pastoral leader, my journey reflects unwavering commitment, sacrifice, and a deep passion for the work of God.

Having witnessed the ministry's growth firsthand under the leadership of Dr. Daniel Kolawole Olukoya, God has been helping me to remain steadfast in advancing the vision of prayer, holiness, and deliverance that defines the church. Now that I am serving at the MFM Prayer City branch in Houston, Texas, God has again positioned me to continue contributing to the ministry's global mission.

Recently, I reflected on my calling into ministry, the challenges and victories of the early days, remarkable testimonies from the fieldwork, and my experience in carrying the fire of revival beyond Nigeria's borders. In all, my story reinforces the importance of being positioned by God for greatness.

From Duty Desk To Divine Assignment

My journey into the ministry began from a deep personal encounter with God and a growing passion for soul-winning and deliverance. I had always been drawn to a life of prayer and service, but my exposure to the teachings and apostolic vision of Dr. Daniel Kolawole Olukoya, the General Overseer of Mountain of Fire and Miracles Ministries, played a major role in shaping my calling.

God strategically positioned me to encounter the great man of God, which became pivotal to the rest of my journey with God and in His service.

The emphasis on holiness, aggressive prayer, and deliverance ministry resonated deeply with me. I saw firsthand how lives were transformed through prayer and the power of God, and that confirmed in my spirit that this was the environment where my calling would find full expression.

Back then, I was operating from the security department where I served as the Information Officer. As a matter of fact, I was the first Information Officer of Mountain of Fire and Miracles Ministries.

My job schedule involved receiving correspondence for the church and for the General Overseer. I would also receive information from the G.O. and pass it on to the appropriate personnel.

At that early stage of the church, office accommodation was still lacking, so my desk was located in the security department, even though my official letter read "Information Officer." A sister colleague we fondly called Mama Aanu served as my supervisor and Chief Information Officer at the time.

However, the ministry later built an office for the information team, where we all moved to fully discharge our duties and responsibilities. Later on, I became a deliverance minister. I was given a promotion letter and subsequently transferred to the Prayer City, where my ministerial responsibilities expanded further.

The Making Of A Ministry In Its Early Fire

I was one of the few who became ministers in the early days of MFM. You may be asking, "What was the experience like then?"

The early days were both challenging and exciting. The ministry was still growing, and many of us had to combine deep spiritual commitment with enormous sacrifice. We spent long hours in prayer, evangelism, and church planting.

Resources were limited compared to what we see today, but the passion for God was extremely intense. The focus was not on structures or numbers but on spiritual power and revival.

Those days shaped us spiritually because we had to rely completely on God for direction, provision, and strength. Looking back now, it was truly a privilege to have been part of those foundational years.

Madness Turned To Miracle

Among many of God's mighty deeds I have seen in my ministry as God continues to position me for His service was the case of a mad man who walked into one of the crusades I organized at Mowe Region in Ogun State and was delivered from his insanity.

That day remains unforgettable. We were holding a crusade in the Mowe region when, during the ministration, a man who had been wandering around the community in a state of insanity suddenly entered the crusade ground.

As prayers were going on, he became very restless and began reacting violently to the prayers. The prayer team and ministers continued to pray fervently, rebuking every power responsible for the affliction.

After some time, the man suddenly became calm. To the amazement of everyone present, he began to speak normally and regained his senses. Members of the community later confirmed that he had been mentally unstable for a long time.

It was a powerful testimony of God's deliverance and a reminder that nothing is impossible with God. When God positions someone, they will experience the greatness of His power.

Faith That Completed A Cathedral

I have also seen God's greatness in provisions for His Kingdom services. I was the pastor who commissioned the ultra-modern Wuye Cathedral in Abuja, Nigeria's Federal Capital Territory, some years ago. People wondered what motivated me to embark on such giant strides, knowing fully well that the edifice had been left uncompleted for many years.

The motivation came from a strong conviction that the work of God must not remain abandoned. When I saw the state of the cathedral project in Wuye, I believed God had a purpose for that structure and for the people who would worship there.

It required faith, persistence, and the collective effort of many dedicated members. We encouraged the congregation to see the project not just as a building, but as a center for revival, prayer, and transformation in the nation's capital.

With prayer, strategic planning, and the sacrificial giving of members, the project gradually moved forward until it was completed and commissioned. Today, it stands as a testimony that with faith and unity, seemingly impossible projects can be accomplished.

Same God, Different Soil

Since coming to the United States of America in another dimension of God's continuous positioning of my ministry for greater service, my experience is unique for many reasons.

Ministering in the United States opened my eyes to the global nature of God's work. While the cultural environment is different from Nigeria, the spiritual needs of people are essentially the same. People everywhere desire purpose, freedom, and a genuine relationship with God.

One thing I observed is that many believers abroad are deeply committed but often face unique challenges such as busy schedules, secular pressures, and cultural distractions.

However, when people encounter the power of God through prayer, teaching, and fellowship, their passion is rekindled. It reinforced my belief that revival is not limited to any nation. Wherever the gospel is preached, and people seek God sincerely, His power is revealed.

Nothing is greater than being where God wants you to be. When God positions you rightly, you begin to experience His power, either as someone He uses or as someone who receives His blessings. Many people miss their purpose in life because they ignore God's direction. Others miss their miracles simply because they are in the wrong place at the right time.

Every level of greatness is connected to divine positioning. Where you stand matters, and when you are there matters even more. This book reminds you that your life can change when you associate yourself with God's plan. May you not miss your moment. May God position you rightly to experience the greatness He has prepared for you.

Chapter 2

Divine Assurance Of Greatness

Daniel sat quietly at the back of a small church one evening. His life did not look like anything close to greatness. He had lost his job months ago. Friends had stopped calling. Even family members had begun to question his decisions. That night, he almost did not attend the service. Something within him still pushed him to go.

As the preacher spoke, one statement caught his attention. It was simple, yet it felt as if it were meant for him. "You are not forgotten. You are only being positioned." Daniel felt something shift inside him. For the first time in months, hope rose again. He went home that night with nothing changed on the outside, but something had changed within him.

Weeks later, an unexpected opportunity came. A job he never applied for opened up to him through someone who barely knew him. Within a short time, his life began to take a new direction. Looking back, Daniel realized something important. His season of struggle was not a sign of failure. It was a stage of positioning.

God Positions Before He Promotes

There is a truth many people do not understand. God does not start with promotion. He starts with positioning. Before anything great appears on the outside, God first arranges things behind the scenes. He aligns events, people, and timing. What looks like a delay is often a divine arrangement.

The Bible says in Jeremiah 29:11, "For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, says the Lord, thoughts of peace and not of evil, to give you a future and a hope." This shows that God already has a plan. Your life is not random. Your journey is not accidental.

You may not understand your present situation, but God understands it fully. He is working with a purpose. Joseph's life is a clear example. He was rejected by his brothers, sold into slavery, and thrown into prison. Yet, none of these stopped the plan of God.

Genesis 50:20 says, "But as for you, you meant evil against me but God meant it for good, in order to bring it about as it is this day, to save many people alive." Joseph's pain was real, but his positioning was greater. What others meant for harm, God turned into a pathway to greatness.

Greatness Is An Act of God

Many people struggle because they think greatness comes only through effort. Effort is important, but it is not the final answer. Greatness is an act of God. It is God's approval upon a life.

Psalm 75:6–7 says, "For exaltation comes neither from the east nor from the west nor from the south. But God is the Judge. He puts down one and exalts another." This means promotion is not controlled by human systems. It is controlled by God.

When God decides to lift a person, no one can stop it. No opinion can block it. No opposition can cancel it. You may feel overlooked today. You may feel forgotten. Yet heaven has not forgotten you. God does not need permission from anyone to bless you. He does not consult people before changing your story. At the right time, He steps in, and everything changes.

Your Present Situation Does Not Define You

It is easy to look at your current situation and conclude that nothing good is coming. That is not true. Your present condition is not your final destination. Romans 8:28 says, "And we know that all things work together for good to those who love God, to those who are the called according to His purpose." Ponder over the phrase all things. Not some things. Even the difficult moments are included.

The setbacks you face are not wasted. The delays you experience are not empty. God is working through them. When there is battle, there is also blessing. When there is difficulty, there is also divine intervention. So do not be discouraged. Do not give up. Do not allow frustration to take over your heart. Something greater is being prepared.

Faith Unlocks Divine Manifestation

Belief is not just a feeling. It is a force that connects you to what God has already prepared. When you believe in the power of God, you align yourself with His purpose. Hebrews 11:1 says, "Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen."

Faith allows you to see beyond your present reality. It helps you hold on when nothing seems to be changing. God responds to faith. He moves when you trust Him. Even when you do not see results immediately, something is happening. God is working quietly. Your tomorrow will be greater than your today. Your future will be better than your past.

Resting In the Assurance of God's Presence

There is nothing to worry about when your life is in God's hands. This is not a statement of denial. It is a statement of trust. It is the quiet confidence that comes from knowing who is in control.

Many people carry heavy burdens because they feel they must figure everything out on their own. They worry about tomorrow, struggle with today, and remain troubled about what they cannot change. Yet the truth remains simple. When your life is in God's hands, you are not alone in your journey.

Isaiah 41:10 says, "Fear not, for I am with you; be not dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you, yes, I will help you, I will uphold you with My righteous right hand." This text means that you are not where you are by accident. God has placed you exactly where you are, in your job, your family, your city, or even your current struggle, because He has a plan to use you in a big way.

Being positioned for greatness could mean being put in a spot that looks scary. Maybe you got a promotion, and you feel like you do not know what you are doing. Maybe you moved to a new city, and you feel lonely. This verse tells us that the position feels less scary because the Commander is right there with us. You are not positioned alone; you are positioned with God.

Chapter 3

The Nature And Manifestation Of Greatness

Sister Grace worked quietly in a small office at the edge of the city. She was diligent, respectful, and consistent, yet she remained unnoticed for years. Others who came after her seemed to move ahead faster. Promotions passed her by. Recognition never came.

Many times, she wondered if her efforts mattered. Still, she chose to remain faithful in her duties. She did her work with excellence, even when no one seemed to be watching.

One morning, everything changed. A major restructuring took place in the organization. Positions shifted. Leaders were replaced. Out of nowhere, Grace was called into a meeting she did not expect. Within a short time, she was elevated into a position that placed her above those who once overlooked her. The same people who had ignored her began to seek her counsel.

As she sat in her new office, one truth became clear to her. Her lifting did not come from human favor alone. It was the hand of God. What she could not achieve by striving, God released by His power.

Greatness Is the Work of God

Greatness is not a human achievement. It is a divine act. It is the visible evidence of God's hand upon a life. Many struggle because they think greatness comes only from effort, connections, or opportunity. These things have their place, but they are not the source.

Psalm 75:6 to 7 says, "For exaltation comes neither from the east nor from the west nor from the south. But God is the Judge, He puts down one and exalts another." This passage makes it clear. Promotion is not controlled by human systems. It is determined by God. When God approves a person, no system can block it. When God lifts someone, no force can pull that person down.

The Meaning Of True Greatness

To become great is not only about status. It is about purpose. It is about living a life that reflects excellence and impact. Greatness means living a life that stands out. It means carrying influence that blesses others.

Genesis 12:2 says, "I will make you a great nation; I will bless you and make your name great; and you shall be a blessing." God did not promise Abraham greatness for personal pride. He promised greatness so that Abraham could become a blessing. This is the true nature of greatness. It flows outward. It touches lives. It changes generations. A person is truly great when others are lifted through their life.

When God Decides To Lift You

There is a moment when God decides to act. When that moment comes, things begin to align in ways that cannot be explained naturally. Doors open without struggle. Opportunities appear without planning. Favor begins to speak where effort once failed.

Isaiah 60:22 says, "A little one shall become a thousand, and a small one a strong nation. I, the Lord, will hasten it in its time." This shows that God can accelerate a life. What takes others many years, God can accomplish in a short time. When God decides to make you great, resistance loses its power. Opposition weakens. Confusion disappears. What once delayed you begins to push you forward.

Divine Protection in Greatness

One fear many people carry is this: What if I rise and fall again? What if I succeed and lose everything? When greatness comes from God, it is preserved by God. Psalm 121:7 says, "The Lord shall preserve you from all evil; He shall preserve your soul." When God lifts you, He also keeps you. He protects your destiny. He guards your future.

There are times when people are mocked, rejected, and misunderstood. It can feel painful and discouraging. Yet God has a way of changing the story. Isaiah 54:17 says, "No weapon formed against you shall prosper, and every tongue which rises against you in judgment you shall condemn." This is not just a promise of protection. It is a promise of vindication.

Those who rise against you will not succeed. The words spoken against you will not stand. When God decides to lift you, even your critics will witness your rising. Joseph experienced this. The same brothers who sold him later stood before him. The one they rejected became the one they depended on. God knows how to turn rejection into honor.

Living in the Reality of Greatness

When you understand that you are positioned by God, your mindset changes. You stop living in fear. You stop living in doubt. You begin to walk in confidence. You understand that your tomorrow will be better than your today. You expect joy. You expect progress. You expect an increase.

Philippians 1:6 says, "Being confident of this very thing, that He who has begun a good work in you will complete it until the day of Jesus Christ." God does not start what He cannot finish. If He has begun something in your life, He will complete it.

You have been positioned for greatness. This is not a wish. It is a reality established by God. Do not worry. Do not be afraid. Do not lose hope. Stand firm in what God has spoken. Trust His process. Wait for His timing. Your tomorrow will be wonderful. Your future will be full of joy. The hand of God is already at work. And when His work becomes visible, no one will be able to deny it.

Chapter 4

God Will Renew Your Hope

Mary sat by her window, staring at the quiet street below. Life had not turned out the way she expected. She had prayed, fasted, and trusted God, yet things seemed to move in the opposite direction. Her business had slowed down. Bills were waiting. Doors she hoped would open had remained closed.

That evening, she picked up her Bible with a tired heart. She did not read much. Her eyes rested on a single verse. "Fear not, for I am with you; be not dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you, yes, I will help you, I will uphold you with My righteous right hand." Isaiah 41:10. She read it again. This time, slowly. Something quiet and deep began to settle in her heart.

She realized that her greatest need was not money or opportunity. Her greatest need was the assurance that God was with her. Days passed. Nothing changed immediately, yet something had changed within her. She stopped living in fear. She began to trust again.

Within weeks, unexpected help came. A friend connected her to a new opportunity. One step led to another. What looked like a closed chapter became the beginning of something new. Mary later understood something she had missed before. God had never left her. He was working, even when she could not see it.

God's Presence Is Your Assurance

Hear the word of God. You will not suffer forever because God is with you. You will not be put to shame because His presence remains with you. Isaiah 43:2 says, "When you pass through the waters, I will be with you; and through the rivers, they shall not overflow you. When you walk through the fire, you shall not be burned."

Notice the language. It does not say if you pass through. It says when you pass through. Challenges will come, yet God promises His presence in the midst of them. His presence changes everything. It gives strength in weakness. It brings peace in confusion. It provides direction in uncertainty. When God is with you, you are never alone. That is enough assurance.

Trials Are Not The End Of The Story

There are moments when life feels unfriendly. Things do not go as planned. Effort does not seem to produce results. In such moments, it is easy to feel discouraged. Yet the word of God teaches us to see beyond the present. James 1:2 to 3 says, "My brethren, count it all joy when you fall into various trials, knowing that the testing of your faith produces patience."

Trials are not meant to destroy you. They are meant to strengthen you. What feels like pressure is often preparation. What feels like a delay is often a development. Something new is coming, even when the present moment feels difficult.

God's intention toward you has never been evil. His plan has always been good from the beginning. Jeremiah 29:11 says, "For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, says the Lord, thoughts of peace and not of evil, to give you a future and a hope." This truth is important. It means your life is not moving toward destruction. It is moving toward purpose.

Guard Your Heart Against Wrong Voices

In difficult seasons, many voices will speak. Some will bring fear. Others will bring doubt. You must choose what you listen to. The enemy speaks through discouragement, lies, and fear. He offers nothing good.

John 10:10 says, "The thief does not come except to steal, and to kill, and to destroy. I have come that they may have life, and that they may have it more abundantly." The enemy has no good intentions. He cannot help you. He only seeks to destroy confidence and faith. So do not listen to voices that weaken your spirit. Stay with the word of God. Let His truth guide your heart.

Faith is what keeps you steady when everything around you feels uncertain. It is not about seeing everything clearly. It is about trusting God completely. Hebrews 11:6 says, "But without faith it is impossible to please Him, for he who comes to God must believe that He is, and that He is a rewarder of those who diligently seek Him." Faith believes that God is present. Faith believes that God will act. When you hold on to faith, you remain connected to God's promises.

Your Season Will Change

No condition remains the same forever. Psalm 30:5 says, "Weeping may endure for a night, but joy comes in the morning." This is a promise of change. Your present situation is temporary. Your season will shift. God has the power to turn things around. What looks difficult today can become a testimony tomorrow. So do not settle in sorrow. Do not remain in despair. Joy is coming.

Freedom and breakthrough are your portion. The word of God declares freedom for His children. John 8:36 says, "Therefore if the Son makes you free, you shall be free indeed." This freedom is complete. It is not partial. It includes freedom from fear, from bondage, from limitation, and from every form of oppression. Your time of healing has come. Your breakthrough time has come. Your time of success has come. God can restore, lift, and establish you.

When God positions you for greatness, barriers begin to fall. Limitations lose their power. Isaiah 45:2 says, "I will go before you and make the crooked places straight; I will break in pieces the gates of bronze and cut the bars of iron." This is what God does. He goes ahead of you. He removes obstacles. He creates a path where there was none. What seemed impossible begins to open. You move forward with ease because God is leading the way.

Chapter 5

God Turns Evil Plans Into Divine Purposes

Samuel never imagined that betrayal would come from someone he trusted. He had worked closely with Daniel, his colleague and friend, for years. They shared ideas, supported each other, and often spoke about their dreams. Samuel believed they were building something meaningful together.

One day, an opportunity came at work. A major project that could change everything. Samuel had quietly prepared for it. He had done the research, developed the strategy, and waited for the right moment to present it. Before he could act, Daniel went ahead of him. He presented Samuel's ideas as his own. The leadership approved it immediately. Samuel was left shocked, hurt, and silent.

For weeks, he struggled within himself. Questions filled his mind. Why did this happen? Why would someone he trusted do this? At one point, he almost gave up. Yet something within him refused to break. He continued to work with integrity. He refused to fight back in anger. He chose to trust God, even when he did not understand.

Months later, the project began to face serious challenges. The foundation was weak. The leadership started asking questions. They needed someone who truly understood the original vision. Samuel was called in. At first, he was hesitant. Still, he stepped forward and explained everything clearly. His depth of understanding became obvious.

Within a short time, he was asked to take over the project. What was taken from him became the very reason he was lifted. That day, as he sat quietly in his office, Genesis 50:20 came alive in his heart. He understood clearly that what was meant to harm him had become the pathway to his elevation. God did not waste the pain. He turned it into a purpose.

What People Intend For Harm Does Not Have The Final Authority

Genesis 50:20 says, "But as for you, you meant evil against me; but God meant it for good, in order to bring it about as it is this day, to save many people alive." This is one of the clearest revelations of divine positioning. Joseph did not deny the evil done against him. He acknowledged it. Yet he also understood something deeper. God was working through it.

This teaches a powerful truth. What people intend for harm does not have the final authority over your life. God does. When God positions a person for greatness, there may be seasons of betrayal, rejection, and delay. Sometimes, even people you trust may turn against you. It can be painful. It can be confusing. Yet none of these can cancel God's purpose.

Romans 8:28 says, "And we know that all things work together for good to those who love God, to those who are the called according to His purpose." All things include the good and the bad. It includes what you understand and what you do not understand. God is able to take every piece of your story and weave it into something meaningful. So do not worry when things seem to go wrong. God is still at work.

God Controls Times and Seasons

Daniel 2:21 says, "And He changes the times and the seasons; He removes kings and raises up kings." This means your life is not at the mercy of circumstances. God controls seasons. A difficult season can change. A closed chapter can open again. A dry period can become fruitful.

What you cannot change, God can change. He can turn sorrow into joy. He can turn delay into acceleration. He can turn confusion into clarity. Ecclesiastes 3:1 says, "To everything there is a season, a time for every purpose under heaven." Your present season is not permanent. If things are hard now, it means you are in a season. And seasons change. God knows how to move you from where you are to where you are supposed to be.

You Were Known and Ordained Before Birth

Jeremiah 1:5 says, "Before I formed you in the womb I knew you; before you were born I sanctified you; I ordained you a prophet to the nations." This is a deep revelation. Your life did not begin at birth. It began in the mind of God. Before you came into this world, God already had a plan for you. He has already assigned a purpose to your life.

Many people struggle because they do not understand this. They look at their present condition and feel lost. They feel confused. Yet the truth is this. Your life is not empty. Your life carries divine intention. Ephesians 2:10 says, "For we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand that we should walk in them." You are God's workmanship. You are not a mistake. You are not an accident. God has already prepared a path for you. Your responsibility is to walk in it. So rise up. See yourself differently. Begin to believe what God has said about you.

You Cannot Be Hidden

Matthew 5:14 says, "You are the light of the world. A city that is set on a hill cannot be hidden." Light is meant to be seen. A city on a hill cannot be covered. This means your greatness cannot remain hidden forever. There may be a season where you feel unnoticed. There may be a time when your efforts are not recognized. Yet that season will not last forever.

When God decides to reveal you, no force can hide you. Isaiah 60:1 says, "Arise, shine; for your light has come! And the glory of the Lord is risen upon you." Your light will shine. Your glory will be seen. No devil can keep you in darkness when God has called you into light.

Isaiah 14:27 says, "For the Lord of hosts has purposed, and who will annul it? His hand is stretched out, and who will turn it back?" When God has decided, it is settled. His power does not fail. His purpose does not change. Numbers 23:19 says, "God is not a man, that He should lie, nor a son of man, that He should repent. Has He said, and will He not do? Or has He spoken, and will He not make it good?" If God has spoken concerning your life, it will come to pass. So relax in His promises. Trust His word. Hold on to His truth.
